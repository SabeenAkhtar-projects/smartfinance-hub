Financial Glossary
Key terms every financially smart person should know
Budget
A plan for how to spend and save money over a specific period, usually monthly.
Net Worth
Total assets minus total liabilities. A snapshot of your overall financial health.
Compound Interest
Interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest — the foundation of wealth building.
Emergency Fund
Money set aside to cover 3–6 months of living expenses in case of unexpected events like job loss or illness.
Inflation
The rate at which the general price level of goods and services rises, reducing the purchasing power of money over time.
Liquidity
How quickly an asset can be converted into cash without significantly affecting its market price.
